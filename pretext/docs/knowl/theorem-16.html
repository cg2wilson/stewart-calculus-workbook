Theorem 3.2.1. Rolle's Theorem.
Let \(f\) be a function that satisfies the following three properties:

  • \(f\) is continuous on the closed interval \([a,b]\text{.}\)

  • \(f\) is differentiable on the open interval \((a,b)\text{.}\)

  • \(f(a) = f(b)\text{.}\)

Then, there is some number \(c\) in \((a,b)\) such that \(f'(c) = 0\text{.}\)

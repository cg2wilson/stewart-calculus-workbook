Using the first definition of the slope of the tangent line, we have

\begin{align*} f'(1) &= \ds \lim_{x\to 1} \dfrac{f(x)-f(1)}{x-1}\\ &= \ds \lim_{x\to 1} \dfrac{x^2-1}{x-1}\\ &= \ds \lim_{x\to 1} \dfrac{(x+1)(x-1)}{x-1}\\ &= \ds \lim_{x\to 1} x+1\\ &= 2 \end{align*}

Using the second definition of the slope of the tangent line, we have

\begin{align*} f'(1) &=\ds \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(1+h)-f(1)}{h}\\ &= \ds \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{(1+h)^2-1}{h}\\ &= \ds \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{1+2h+h^2-1}{h}\\ &= \ds \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{2h+h^2}{h}\\ &= \ds \lim_{h\to 0} 2+h\\ &= 2 \end{align*}

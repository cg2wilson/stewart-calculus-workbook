Based on the region, we can write
\begin{equation*} R = \lrbrace{(r,\theta)\mid 1\leq r\leq 2, 0\leq \theta\leq \dfrac{\pi}{2}} \end{equation*}
so the integral becomes
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi/2}\int_1^2 (1-r^2\cos^2\theta)\, r\,dr\,d\theta \end{equation*}
which evaluates to \(-\dfrac{3\pi}{16}\)

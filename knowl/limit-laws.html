Theorem 1.6.1. Limit Laws.
Suppose that \(c\) is a constant, and that the limits \(\ds \lim_{x\to a} f(x)\) and \(\ds \lim_{x\to a}\) exist. Then,
  1. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{x\to a} [f(x) \pm g(x)] = \ds \lim_{x\to a} f(x) \pm \lim_{x\to a} g(x)\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{x\to a} [cf(x)] = c\cdot \lim_{x\to a} f(x)\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{x\to a} [f(x)\cdot g(x)] = \lim_{x\to a} f(x)\cdot \lim_{x\to a} g(x)\)
  4. \(\ds \lim_{x\to a} \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \dfrac{\ds \lim_{x\to a} f(x)}{\ds \lim_{x\to a} g(x)}\text{,}\) provided that \(\ds \lim_{x\to a} g(x)\neq 0\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{x\to a} [f(x)]^n = \left[\lim_{x\to a} f(x)\right]^n\)
  6. \(\displaystyle \ds \lim_{x\to a} c = c\)
  7. \(\ds \lim_{x\to a} [f(x)]^{1/n} = \left[\lim_{x\to a} f(x)\right]^{1/n}\text{,}\) where \(n\) is a positive integer (we assume the limit is positive if \(n\) is even)

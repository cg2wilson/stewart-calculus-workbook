Item.
If the power of cosine is odd:
\begin{align*} \ds \int \sin^mx\cos^{2k+1}x\, dx \amp = \int\sin^m x\cos^{2k}x\cdot \cos x\, dx\\ \amp = \ds \int \sin^m x (\cos^2x)^k\cos x\, dx\\ \amp = \ds \int \sin^m x (1-\sin^2x)^k\cos x\, dx \end{align*}
Now set \(u = \sin x\text{,}\) simplify, and integrate.

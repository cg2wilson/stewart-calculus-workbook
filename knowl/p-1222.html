At \(t = \pm 1\text{,}\) the curve passes through \((2,0)\) so we know that the curve has up to two tangent lines at that point. Then, \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{3t^2-1}{4t}\text{;}\) evaluating at \(t = 1\) gives \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{1}{2}\) and at \(t = -1\) gives \(\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{1}{2}\text{.}\)

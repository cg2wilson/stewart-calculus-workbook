Theorem 4.2.13. Properties of the Definite Integral.
Let \(f,g\) be continuous functions on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Let \(c\) be a constant. Then,
  1. \(\displaystyle \ds \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = -\int_b^a f(x)\, dx\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \ds \int_a^a f(x)\,dx = 0\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \ds \int_a^b c\, dx = c(b-a)\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \ds \int_a^b [f(x)\pm g(x)]\,dx = \int_a^b f(x)\,dx\pm \int_a^b g(x)\,dx\)
  5. \(\displaystyle \int_a^b cf(x)\,dx = c\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\)
  6. \(\ds \int_a^b f(x)\, dx = \int_a^d f(x)\, dx + \int_d^b f(x)\, dx\text{,}\) for some \(a \lt d \lt b\)

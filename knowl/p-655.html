Let \(y = \tan x\text{.}\) Then, \(x = \tan y\text{.}\) Taking the implicit derivative, we have
\begin{equation*} 1 = \sec^2 y \dfrac{dy}{dx} \end{equation*}
From the Pythagorean Theorem, we know that \(\sec^2 y = 1+\tan^2y\text{.}\) Since \(x=\tan y\text{,}\) we conclude that \(\sec^2y = 1 + x^2\)
